01The problem

No one watches where scams are born

Banks monitor transactions. Telcos block calls. But the phishing sites, fake shops, fake investment platforms and brand-impersonating domains where scams originate? Nobody watches that layer systematically.

05Clone & brand protection · Live

Catching impersonation before victims do

Clone-watch
Daily sweep of newly-registered domains against a watchlist of Australian brands — surfaces look-alike domains the day they appear.
Certificate Transparency
Monitors public TLS-certificate logs (crt.sh) for certificates issued to brand-impersonating domains.
Shop Signal
Flags fake or high-risk online shops at scan time, with optional paid commerce-reputation enrichment.
Brand notification
Routes confirmed impersonations to each brand's security / fraud channel (security.txt, VDP, fraud inbox) for takedown.
In production The first live brand notification — to a major Australian bank — was sent in May 2026. This is the platform's direct contribution to the SPF Prevent principle.

09Scams Prevention Framework

Built for the 1 July 2026 deadline

A$52.7M
max penalty per obligation

The SPF Act (Royal Assent Feb 2025) obliges 200+ banks, telcos and digital platforms to Prevent · Detect · Disrupt · Report scams. Ask Arthur maps directly to these obligations.

Prevent
Certificate-Transparency & clone-watch alerts on brand-impersonating domains; real-time consumer warnings.
Detect
20+ feeds + community reports as external scam intelligence; domain aggregation surfaces emerging campaigns.
Disrupt
Actionable intelligence via API drives URL blocking and customer warnings; entity risk scores power decisions.
Report
Trending-threats endpoint + government-ready exports + onward reporting to regulators (potential AFCX feed).
Plus a free in-product SPF self-assessment (SPF / DMARC / DKIM email-auth health check) that any organisation can run today at askarthur.au.
