# Ask Arthur — Executive Summary

## Founder's Note

Australians lost $2.18 billion to scams last year (NASC 2024). The number has grown every year since tracking began. The people most at risk — older Australians, non-native English speakers, people in crisis — are the least equipped to identify increasingly sophisticated AI-generated scam messages.

On 21 February 2025, the Scams Prevention Framework Act received Royal Assent. From 1 July 2026, banks, telcos, and digital platforms face legally enforceable obligations to detect, report, and disrupt scams — or face penalties of up to $52.7 million per breach. Over 200 regulated entities must comply. No incumbent vendor dominates the cross-sector threat intelligence layer this legislation demands.

Ask Arthur is Australia's first community-sourced scam intelligence platform. We give every Australian a free, instant way to check if a message is a scam — and we turn every check into threat intelligence that protects everyone else.

## The Product

**Consumer (Free)**: Paste a suspicious message, URL, screenshot, or QR code into any of 7 surfaces (web app, Chrome extension, mobile app, Telegram, WhatsApp, Slack, Messenger) and get an instant AI verdict with plain-language explanation, red flags, and next steps. No signup, no data stored.

**B2B (Paid)**: Real-time Threat Intelligence API for banks, telcos, and digital platforms. 6 endpoints providing aggregated, PII-scrubbed threat data — trending scams, URL intelligence with WHOIS/SSL enrichment, entity risk scores, and bulk lookups. Directly enables Scams Prevention Framework compliance.

**Security Scanner**: Unified multi-type scanner covering websites, Chrome extensions, MCP servers, and AI skills — graded A+ to F with embeddable badges.

## The Moat: Data Velocity

Every consumer check enriches our threat database. Every threat in the database improves our detection. This creates a **data flywheel** that accelerates with scale:

```
More users -> More checks -> More verified threats -> Better detection -> More users
                                    |
                          B2B threat intelligence
                          (banks, telcos, platforms)
```

No competitor has this loop. Scamwatch relies on manual reports. Banks see only their own customers' data. BioCatch is banking-only. Quantium Telstra is proprietary. We see the full picture, in real-time, across all channels and sectors.
