## 2. Purpose

The Parties wish to explore a potential business relationship relating to scam detection, cybersecurity, or related services ("Purpose"). In connection with the Purpose, each Party may disclose Confidential Information to the other.

---

## 3. Definitions

**"Confidential Information"** means any non-public information disclosed by one Party ("Disclosing Party") to the other Party ("Receiving Party"), whether orally, in writing, electronically, or by any other means, that:

- (a) is marked or designated as "confidential", "proprietary", or similar; or
- (b) by its nature or the circumstances of disclosure, would reasonably be understood to be confidential.

Confidential Information includes, without limitation:

- Technical information: source code, algorithms, APIs, system architecture, security controls, threat intelligence data, scam detection methodologies, model configurations;
- Business information: pricing, customer lists, financial data, business plans, roadmaps, marketing strategies;
- Data: personal information, usage data, analytics, performance metrics;
- Legal information: contracts, terms, compliance documentation.

---

## 4. Exclusions

Confidential Information does not include information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate:

- (a) **Public domain:** Was or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Receiving Party;
- (b) **Prior knowledge:** Was already known to the Receiving Party without restriction prior to disclosure, as evidenced by written records;
- (c) **Independent development:** Was independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information, as evidenced by written records;
- (d) **Third-party disclosure:** Was rightfully received from a third party without restriction on disclosure and without breach of any obligation of confidentiality;
- (e) **Required by law:** Is required to be disclosed by law, regulation, or valid court order, provided the Receiving Party gives the Disclosing Party prompt written notice (to the extent legally permitted) and reasonable opportunity to seek protective measures.

---

## 5. Obligations

5.1. The Receiving Party shall:

- (a) Hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence;
- (b) Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party except as permitted by this Agreement;
- (c) Use Confidential Information solely for the Purpose;
- (d) Protect Confidential Information with at least the same degree of care it uses for its own confidential information, but in no event less than reasonable care;
- (e) Limit access to Confidential Information to its employees, contractors, and professional advisers ("Representatives") who have a need to know and are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as this Agreement.

5.2. The Receiving Party is responsible for any breach of this Agreement by its Representatives.

---

## 6. Permitted Disclosures

6.1. Either Party may disclose the existence of this Agreement (but not its terms or the Confidential Information exchanged) without the other Party's consent.

6.2. Confidential Information may be disclosed to a Party's legal, financial, and tax advisers who are bound by professional confidentiality obligations.

6.3. Either Party may disclose Confidential Information with the prior written consent of the Disclosing Party.

---

## 7. No Rights Granted

7.1. All Confidential Information remains the property of the Disclosing Party. Nothing in this Agreement grants the Receiving Party any licence, right, or interest in the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information, intellectual property, or other proprietary rights.

7.2. Neither Party is obligated to disclose any particular information or to enter into any further agreement.

---

## 8. Return and Destruction

8.1. Upon written request of the Disclosing Party, or upon expiration or termination of this Agreement, the Receiving Party shall promptly:

- (a) Return or destroy all Confidential Information in its possession (including copies, notes, and summaries); and
- (b) Provide written certification of destruction within 14 days.

8.2. The Receiving Party may retain one archival copy of Confidential Information solely for legal compliance purposes, subject to the ongoing confidentiality obligations of this Agreement.

---

## 9. Term

9.1. **Agreement Term.** This Agreement is effective from the date of the last signature below and remains in force for a period of **2 years** unless terminated earlier by either Party upon 30 days written notice.

9.2. **Survival.** The confidentiality obligations in this Agreement survive expiration or termination and continue for a period of 2 years after the date of disclosure of the relevant Confidential Information.

---

## 10. Remedies

10.1. Each Party acknowledges that a breach of this Agreement may cause irreparable harm for which monetary damages would be inadequate. Accordingly, either Party may seek injunctive or other equitable relief in addition to any other remedies available at law or in equity.

10.2. The rights and remedies in this Agreement are cumulative and not exclusive of any other rights or remedies.
