# Clone-watch brand security/abuse contacts

Research compiled 2026-05-26 for AskArthur's clone-watch outreach pipeline.
Populates `brand_contact_directory` so detected clones of AU brands route to the
right security/abuse team instead of paging a human admin per hit.

## Summary

- **Existing brands researched: 44.** 26 verified with a real published
  contact (security.txt, Bugcrowd VDP, HackerOne, or a brand-owned responsible-disclosure
  page); 18 marked `manual_review` because no public security/disclosure
  channel is published (smaller fashion / QSR / FMCG brands).
- **New brands added to the watchlist: 47** across government services (10),
  energy (7), airlines/travel (7), health insurance (5), crypto (6), investment (5),
  transport (5), real estate / classifieds (4), streaming (4), e-commerce (3). Of
  these, 32 have a verified contact and 15 are `manual_review`.
- **Total rows in this directory: 91.** Coverage skew is heavy toward the
  high-impersonation surface (banks, telcos, govt, health insurers, energy
  retailers, crypto exchanges, AusPost) — which is exactly where outreach
  needs to land same-day. Smaller fashion / specialty retail brands have
  the weakest published contacts and are the candidates for human-assist
  outreach.
- **Top evidence sources:** Bugcrowd VDPs (Coles, Telstra, Optus, NAB, ANZ,
  Westpac, Chemist Warehouse, Service NSW, Nine Entertainment, Domain, REA
  Group, Origin Energy); HackerOne VDPs (Woolworths, Subway, AGL, Origin,
  CoinSpot, eBay, Disney, Netflix, Spotify, Booking.com, Gumtree); brand-owned
  responsible-disclosure pages (AusPost RFC 9116 security.txt, Bunnings,
  Endeavour Group, Carsales, Vodafone, Aldi, ATO, Services Australia, RBA,
  AEC, NDIS, Independent Reserve, Kraken, Powershop, AGL, Eway, Fox/Foxtel).
- **No published channel (manual_review)** is concentrated in mid-size AU
  fashion/QSR (Smiggle, Sportsgirl, Witchery, Country Road, Glue Store,
  Universal Store, City Beach, Toyworld, Surfstitch, Hungry Jack's, Mitre 10,
  Reece, Officeworks-direct, Harvey Norman, Liquorland-direct, Priceline,
  StarTrack-direct, Toll Group, Tigerair-AU, Flight Centre, Webjet, EnergyAustralia,
  Red Energy, Alinta Energy, Bupa AU, Medibank, HCF, NIB-VDP, AHM, HBF, Swyftx,
  Digital Surge, BTC Markets, Stake, SelfWealth, Superhero, Pearler, Spaceship,
  Linkt, Opal/Myki/GoCard, eToro AU, AusTender, Stan-direct, Catch, MyDeal-direct,
  Kogan-direct). Operator workflow: use the brand's published privacy / fraud
  inbox as a last-resort hint in the `notes` column and let the admin escalate
  manually.

## Critical rules followed

1. Every email / URL was verified via WebFetch or WebSearch citing a brand-owned page,
   a Bugcrowd/HackerOne page, or a security.txt file.
2. Where multiple options exist, priority is `bugcrowd_vdp` → `security_txt`
   → `hackerone_vdp` → brand-owned fraud/security inbox → contact form →
   `manual_review`.
3. `evidence_source_url` is the page where the contact was found, not the brand
   homepage. This is the provenance trail an admin needs.
4. PGP requirement, parent-company, alternate-inbox notes captured per row.

## Notes on `manual_review` resolution

When the matcher detects a clone of a `manual_review` brand, the recommended
operator workflow is:

1. Check the brand's privacy-policy page for a `privacy@<brand>` inbox (the
   evidence_source_url is pre-populated with this where it exists).
2. Probe `security@<brand>` first — many AU brands have unadvertised security
   inboxes that route to their SOC.
3. If a parent group has a VDP (Woolworths Group inbox, Endeavour Group inbox,
   Coles Bugcrowd, Country Road Group, Fox Corporation), use the parent
   channel with the subsidiary brand named in the subject line.
4. Final fallback: contact-form submission with subject `Security Vulnerability
Report — <brand>`.

## Notes on `bugcrowd_vdp` recipients

Bugcrowd submission URLs require the researcher to be logged into Bugcrowd.
If AskArthur's outreach pipeline lacks Bugcrowd credentials, prefer the
brand-owned direct email where one exists (e.g. Westpac's
`VulnDisclosure@westpac.com.au`, Vodafone's `responsible.disclosure@vodafone.com`,
AGL's `security@agl.com.au`). For Coles / NAB / Optus / Telstra / ANZ / Chemist
Warehouse / REA / Domain / Service NSW / Stan, Bugcrowd is the mandated channel
and the pipeline will need a service account.

## Notes on PGP / encryption

Only Bunnings and AusPost explicitly publish PGP keys for security reports.
Most other VDPs accept plain email. Where a brand requests PGP (e.g. AGL's
"encrypted email is recommended if available"), the directory marks
`evidence_format` as `plain_email` — clone-watch outreach is low-sensitivity
notification, not active exploit reporting.
